Red Scale: A fun little film project I was apart of in early 2021.

One thing that is important to me is being grounded and align with myself, this is something that is easily overlooked when life gets busy. To keep the feeling of being grounded I dedicate time to the things that I love, this includes; spending time with my loved ones, making sure my space is clean and tidy, going to the beach, putting my records on and of course, using my film camera. So I joined a film club in Geelong and its been great!!

The process of film club is that each month you receive a theme to follow, take your time to capture images within said theme and meet up to show your results. At the end of each meeting you receive a new theme and go again. One theme I loved in particular is red scale.

Red scale is the process of purchasing a brand new roll of film and cutting off the beginning of the roll that is curved so that the roll is completely straight. You then find an empty canister of film, and tape together the straight end of the film from the new canister to the little bit of film left in the empty canister. From there you push both canisters together so no light will expose the film, and wind the film from the new canister into the old one. Once the roll of film is completely wound in you cut in-between each canister to seperate - but be careful not to cut any sprockets because the film wont be able to be processed. Lastly you take the beginning of the film you cut off previously and use that as a guide to cut a curve at the new start of the roll.

A roll of film is layered with different colours, starting from yellow to blues to magentas. When winding the roll backwards it changes the order of the colour, therefore when the light hits the film when a photo is taken, it hits the red colours first creating a red scale image.I had so much fun capturing my images as I had no idea how they were going to turn out, my inspiration ranged from an art gallery, the beach and the streets of Lorne. I love photogrpahy, and this keeps the fun in things.
